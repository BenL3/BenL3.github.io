Project Description

Blind people today have a hard time navigating the world around them. Unless they have memorized the environments that they frequent often, they have no good way to navigate independently. They would either need to purchase a service animal or have a caretaker or friend take care of them. Service animals are very expensive and not every blind or visually impaired individual can afford that luxury. Our group is hoping to address this problem with our project. We are building a solution that blind people can use to regain their independence and be able to explore brand new environments. We are building a pair of wearable eyewear that a blind person can use that will be able to tell them critical information about the world around them through auditory cues. It will allow them to know where they are via readouts of street signs and storefront signs, audio prompts of what state traffic lights at (red, yellow, green), warnings of where crosswalks are, and more. Our project will be able to service both the blind and visually impaired to be able for the first time have a much higher sense of security and independence.
